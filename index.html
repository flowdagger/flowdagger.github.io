FlowDAgger: Human-in-the-Loop Adaptation of Generative Robot Policies in Latent Space

Anonymous Authors
Under review at CoRL 2026

Abstract

Pretrained generative robot policies built on flow-matching and diffusion have achieved impressive results across a wide range of manipulation tasks. Yet in practice, deployments routinely expose failure modes outside their pretraining distribution. Closing these gaps either demands large-scale data collection or requires online reinforcement learning on physical hardware, which is impractical and unsafe. We present FlowDAgger, a sample- and compute-efficient method that brings human-in-the-loop supervision into the latent space of frozen flow-matching and diffusion policies. Our key idea is action inversion: each human expert action is mapped back to the noise that would have produced it under the frozen base policy, via reverse-time integration and local refinement. This inverted noise serves as supervision for a lightweight latent policy that steers the base model at deployment, enabling rapid skill acquisition while retaining its behavioral priors. We evaluate FlowDAgger in simulation and on real-world bimanual and single-arm setups, adapting both action-head VLAs and world-action models from a handful of interventions. FlowDAgger outperforms fine-tuning and latent-space RL baselines, offering a highly practical path for adapting generative robot policies in the real world.
